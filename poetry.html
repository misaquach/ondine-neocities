He represents the finest elements of the Romantic era – with his rich imagery of succulent, dew-laden nature and haunting, lonely summer nights; his inexplicable sorrows and contemplations of the brevity of life, and his references to empires and gods of old. “Ode to a Nightingale” and “Ode to a Grecian Urn” together embody my life and soul.
