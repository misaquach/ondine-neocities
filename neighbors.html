Neighboring websites

https://satyrs.eu/
The website of Xanthe, an erudite and affable polytheist from the U.K. They have a deep interest in the world around them — geography, culture, historical linguistics, exploring the world both online and offline. Beautiful decoration as well.

http://foundalis.com/
A fellow from Greece named Harry who discusses physics and math, and teaches his readers basic Greek. I find the website very in the spirit of the Old Web, and I could listen to him ramble about quantum physics all day.

https://alicebluef0f8ff.neocities.org/
This website feels like wandering through a labyrinthine museum whose winding halls are filled with strange and interesting art exhibitions.

https://lovevy.neocities.org/
This website-building is simply amazing — very unique and artistically expressive. A fellow classic literature fan.

https://saint-images.neocities.org/
What I admire about this fellow is their passion for photography. It betrays their keen eye for art and beauty in the world around them — in ordinary life, they’re always picking up on pretty color palettes, nice lighting, shapely scenery. It’s a sharp contrast to my personality — I often get so lost in thought, I don't notice anything around me.

https://notprincehamlet.neocities.org/
A Russian fellow named Marina who mainly discusses postcards, books, and video-games. The design of her website is unique and artistic; it really ties together all the aspects of her personality and interests.

https://saltydracon.neocities.org/
Executes the theme of a city far better than I do. Generally very artistic and interesting.

https://mism4tchedsocks.neocities.org/
A writer, blogger and artist. Great pages about art history — it’s always nice to see people discussing the high arts in an ordinary setting rather than treating it like some obscure, pretentious topic.

https://rini.neocities.org/
A musician from Malaysia who blogs about her life as a violinist.

https://pavel-fyodorovich.neocities.org/
More Dostoyevsky fans!!!! <3 Unfortunately, its owners seem to have abandoned it, but their fanart is very good.

https://maya.land/
A fellow from Seattle who writes about stationery, books, art, and other topics.

https://unfortunateaccident.neocities.org/
The website of Yue is more personal, showing her growth through life in poetry, journaling, and photography. I like her honesty and self-awareness, and some observations she’s made about the world have stayed with me for years.

https://auberylis.moe/
The website of an electronic musician named Aubery who just generally seems very fun and interesting as a person, despite being my spiritual and artistic opposite. Bright, beautiful maximalist style.

https://gbull.neocities.org/
Greenland

https://umbritzer.neocities.org/
The website of Umbritzer, another Neocities book reviewer.

https://suboptimalism.neocities.org/
Insightful writing on a variety of topics such as travel, the Internet, and literature. The fellow has a great taste in books
